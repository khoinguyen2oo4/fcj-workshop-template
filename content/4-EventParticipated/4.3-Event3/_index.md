# Report on “AWS GameDay - Battle of the 8 Teams”

### Event Objectives
- Create a practical playground to directly apply learned AWS knowledge to solve real-world system problems.
- Practice troubleshooting skills and architecture optimization under time pressure.
- Enhance teamwork through competitive head-to-head matches among 8 teams.

### Organization Format
- **Scale:** An intense competition among the 8 most outstanding teams.
- **Format:** Teams compete for points by solving simulated system failures (break/fix), deploying infrastructure upon request, and quickly answering in-depth architectural quizzes.

### Key Highlights

#### Real-world Troubleshooting Challenges (Break/Fix)
- Systems are subjected to simulated failure scenarios (e.g., EC2 crashes, VPC network disconnections, IAM permission errors).
- Teams must quickly read logs, isolate the issue, and recover services (Disaster Recovery) in the shortest time possible to get the system back online and avoid losing points.

#### System Optimization
- From a basic architecture, teams are required to scale up the system to handle heavy loads during sudden traffic spikes.
- Make decisions on selecting appropriate services to ensure both Performance and Cost optimization.

#### Conquering Architectural Scenario Quizzes
- Rapidly answering real-world enterprise scenario questions to review deep understanding of AWS Core Services.

### What I Learned

#### Sharp Mindset & Problem Solving
- Do not panic when the system throws errors. Apply a layer-by-layer review mindset (Network -> Security -> Compute -> Database) to find the Root Cause.
- Learn how to make quick and accurate decisions under the pressure of a countdown timer.

#### Teamwork Skills
- Clear division of labor: someone checks logs, someone configures infrastructure, someone searches documentation.
- Concise communication skills, continuous status reporting (sync-up) so the whole team grasps the progress without stepping on each other's toes.

#### Reinforcing Knowledge of AWS Services
- Clearly distinguish the Use-cases of each service type (When to use Relational DB, when to use NoSQL).
- Gain a deeper understanding of how to configure High Availability and Auto Scaling in a production environment.

### Experience During the Event

Participating in the **“GameDay - Battle of the 8 Teams”** event was one of the most explosive and memorable experiences during my internship. Unlike self-studying or doing labs alone, GameDay pushed the atmosphere to a climax with a constantly changing Leaderboard.

#### "Brain-straining" but Extremely Exciting Feeling
- Competing for every point with the other teams requires high concentration. The feeling when the whole team cheers for successfully fixing a tricky network configuration error is truly amazing.

#### Making Sharp Architectural Decisions
- The most memorable experience was the scenario quiz section. Using our trained knowledge base, the team could analyze keywords extremely fast. 
- A prime example is the scenario in **Set 3 (Bộ Đề 3)**: It required finding a flexible, fast, and highly scalable non-relational database service to handle unpredictable traffic for a Startup's Mobile App. Based on the keywords *"flexible"* and *"scalable non-relational database"*, the correct answer, **Amazon DynamoDB**, was given immediately with full confidence.
