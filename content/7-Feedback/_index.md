### Overall Evaluation

**1. Practical Roadmapt**  
The 12-week experience of the First Cloud AI Journey is an incredibly smooth transition from theory to practice. Instead of vaguely cramming individual AWS services, the program forces learners to see the big picture. The feeling of personally planning, setting up foundational permissions, and then connecting the chain of EC2, Lambda, API Gateway to CloudFront and WAF in the ZeroBug Agent Workshop makes things much clearer. It helps a 4th-year student untangle systemic thinking and realize how Enterprise services interact seamlessly with each other in a production environment.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
The mentor provides very detailed guidance, explains clearly when I don’t understand, and always encourages me to ask questions. The admin team supports administrative tasks, provides necessary documents, and creates favorable conditions for me to work effectively. I especially appreciate that the mentor allows me to try and solve problems myself instead of just giving the answer.

**3. Documentation & Teamwork Operation**  
Biggest challenge: The core problem. Designing the network architecture and breaking down IAM permissions so that all members have enough room to act, sharing the infrastructure smoothly without creating security loopholes, was extremely tough.

Practical obstacle: Synchronizing environmental parameters among overlapping hands is very easy to disrupt if the handover is off by even a single line of config.

Experience: You must lock down the Naming Convention from the very first second. The deployment plan must be transparent, and the team must continuously "sync" with each other to squash bugs as soon as they appear.

**4. Difficulties & Experiences**  
During the internship, I learned many new skills such as using project management tools, teamwork skills, and professional communication within a corporate environment. The mentors also shared numerous practical experiences to help me better navigate my career path.

**5. Support from the Program**  
I want to thank all the seniors for their dedicated support. Their assistance helped the whole team reduce difficulties from start to finish to complete the internship.

### Additional Questions
- What did you find **most satisfying** during your internship?  
It was the dedication of the AWS seniors. Whenever we were stuck on the architecture, they didn't just help fix the error but also pointed out the root cause in an extremely easy-to-understand way. The most important thing was getting into a real system, working according to a standard corporate workflow.
- What do you think the company **should improve** for future interns?  
I think if there's anything to improve, it would be the Event (May 23rd). The reason is that the room was so crowded there weren't enough chairs, so we had to move to the 36th floor. However, I was one of those who got stuck sitting on the floor without a chair, so my experience during the 3-hour lecture was literally a backache, numb legs, and tired eyes. Although I could hear the lecture, my experience was genuinely not pleasant.
- If recommending to a friend, would you **suggest they intern here**? Why or why not?  
YES. For anyone who wants to learn about Cloud and needs a systematic launchpad, simultaneously learning the mindset and getting hands-on experience in a real project to gain practical exposure, this is the perfect destination.
